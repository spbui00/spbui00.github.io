Education in Vietnam

Many Vietnamese students will agree that mathematics was a dominant subject in our education. We extensively practiced various algebraic concepts repeatedly. However, this approach didn't necessarily foster creativity; it primarily aimed to improve fluency. Personally, I developed a strong interest in math, actively solving problems and participating in competitions, achieving commendable results.

In contrast, subjects like History and Geography were notably absent in my education. Upon arriving in Europe, I was introduced to these subjects, which I found to be a significant gap in my knowledge.

High School Period

When I moved to the Czech Republic, I had to start learning the Czech language from scratch. It took me about a year to become fluent in the language. During this period, I attended a lower grade in an elementary school to immerse myself in the language and improve my skills. After two years, I took the entrance exams and was accepted into a Gymnasium, which is a higher level high school in Czechia.

During my initial year at Gymnasium, I had a remarkable teacher who emphasized the importance of competitions, specifically the Math Olympiad. She motivated me to take part, and I successfully cleared the home round, leading me to participate in the regional round. To my delight, I secured the first position in my age group at the regional level, which was the last level in this cathegory. Unfortunately, my teacher later relocated to another school, and I discontinued my involvement in the Math Olympiad thereafter.

The COVID-19 pandemic coincided with my final two years of high school. It was during this period that I decided to embark on an entrepreneurial journey, starting my own business teaching Czech to Vietnamese individuals. I devoted a significant amount of time and energy to its growth, and at times, my focus on academics wavered. Nevertheless, I still managed to complete high school with above-average scores. However, my study methodology took a different direction during this period. I became heavily reliant on spontaneous problem-solving, often neglecting the need for structured learning and a deeper understanding of certain subjects, which left gaps in my knowledge and hindered my overall academic growth.

Technical Computer Science

Wanting to live independently and pursue studies in English, I made the decision to study abroad. After doing some thorough research, I ultimately chose the Netherlands as my destination. The concept of cycling as a primary means of transportation greatly appealed to me, and what truly captivated me was the University of Twente. Their academic program, particularly the opportunity to pursue a double degree in Math and Computer Science, made my decision easier than ever since I always wanted to study Mathematics and learn to code. For me, Math is like the universal language of the universe and programming is like a gateway to realizing our goals and bringing ideas to life.

During my first year, I faced the consequences of my study habits from high school, and my grades fell short of my expectations. It was a wake-up call for me. I realized that I needed to change my approach to studying and better way to balance my work and study. I decided to adopt a more organized and disciplined study routine that would help me improve my efficiency and truly understand the subjects I was studying. This period also gave me the opportunity to meet individuals who inspired and motivated me.

To my surprise, reading about the subjects turned out to be far from boring. In fact, I found it incredibly interesting each time I delved deeper into the topics. I usually do not attend lectures but read textbooks, and only consult the proffesors if I had questions. I also do more of tutorial exercises to get a better grasp of the subject. After every module, we have to work on a project relating to the courses taugh during that module. These projects give us hands-on experience and allow us to apply what we've learned in a practical way. This hands-on approach is one of the aspects I truly value about our school. It not only helps us develop practical skills but also provides valuable insights into teamwork and proper research conduct. Through collaborative projects, I have learned the importance of effective communication, coordination, and division of tasks within a team. Additionally, the emphasis on research has taught me how to conduct thorough investigations, analyze information critically, and present findings in a structured and organized manner. Words cannot describe how amazed I am with the knowledge I gained during these first two years.
